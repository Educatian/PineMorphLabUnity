# PineMorph Lab Evidence-Centered Design

## Competency model

| Construct | Claim about the learner | Observable evidence | Mechanic | Signal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Structure-function reasoning | Explains passive hygromorphic actuation | Identifies differential swelling across bonded layers | 3D mechanism inspection | `tutorial_step` |
| Mechanical prediction | Predicts the limiting actuator outcome | Commits an outcome before results appear | Five prediction cycles | `prediction_selected` |
| Coupled-model reasoning | Connects strain, equilibrium, diffusion, and stress | Uses the mechanics trace and three metrics | Synchronized run sequence | `trial_completed` |
| Constrained optimization | Balances angle, response, and stress | Revises Trial 5 until every limit is met | Independent final design | `trial_completed` |
| Evidence-based justification | Supports a recommendation with data and mechanics | Selects a balanced claim, complete evidence, and causal reasoning | CER evidence check | `assessment_selected` |

## Task model

| Trial | Focal comparison | Purpose |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 1 | Fibers aligned with hinge | Establish an under-opening baseline |
| 2 | Fiber angle changed | Isolate transformed hygroscopic strain |
| 3 | Thick active layer | Expose the diffusion-time trade-off |
| 4 | Extreme stiffness mismatch | Expose stress and over-opening risk |
| 5 | All inputs learner-controlled | Demonstrate independent three-constraint optimization |

## Learning argument

Correct predictions alone are insufficient evidence. The learner must use opening angle, t95 response time, and peak stress with their units and limits, then connect those values to the strain-equilibrium-diffusion chain. The final trial removes recommended settings and retains only the constraint lines.

## Interpretation limit

The in-app evidence check summarizes performance inside this instructional model. It is not an externally validated measure of general mechanical-engineering proficiency.
